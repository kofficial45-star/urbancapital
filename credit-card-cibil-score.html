What to know about Credit Card CIBIL Score

Credit card approval depends heavily on credit score, repayment behavior, income, bureau history, and issuer rules. A good score improves chances but does not guarantee approval.

  • A score around 700+ is commonly preferred, while premium cards may need stronger profiles.
  • Recent delays, high utilization, and too many enquiries can reduce approval chances.
  • New-to-credit customers may need entry-level, secured, or FD-backed cards.
  • Always compare annual fee, rewards, and eligibility before applying.

Best way to proceed

Do not apply randomly everywhere. First compare eligibility, document readiness, CIBIL impact, fees, and repayment comfort. Then submit a guided enquiry only when you are ready.
